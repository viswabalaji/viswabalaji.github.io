Creola sipped the sweet bubble tea, her head and feet dangling in unison to the soft music in the library. The rustic binding and fragrance of physical books reminded her of her father's library, the only relic of his past. She heaved a sigh of relief as her eyes rolled over the last paragraph of the book. The adventure thriller had come to an end with a delightful marriage of the protagonists who lived happily ever after.

She replaced the book on the right shelf, stretched her tense muscles, and packed her bags. Her eyes caught a glance of a young man staring at her. He was well dressed in a light pink shirt, summer shorts, and sport shoes. Curls of hair bounced around his neck. His fingers revealed a hint of nervousness right before he thrust them into his pockets.

His heart raced as he approached her with a forced grin on his pale face. "Hey Creola, I know you from our piano class. Mrs. Gerard never fails to mention your talent during my lessons, which is right after yours on Saturday evening. I heard a lot about you and like you a lot. Hope this is not too forward of me to ask, but could we maybe... uh... sometime go out for coffee?"

She had been an introvert all her life. Bed, books, music was her holy trinity. She did not like being put in the spotlight with a stranger. Her mother's warning from earlier flashed through her mind, "For your zodiac sign today, speak to no stranger today for they will bring great downfall to the family." Her mother had taken to astrology with steadfast loyalty since her father had passed. Creola, though an educated woman, put on a charade of acceptance for her mother. "A frail stick is a god given message to the drowning man", she reasoned. She did not want to break the twig.

Like a communicable disease, her heartbeat increased, she felt dizzy, nervous, and could not stop blushing. No man had ever approached her in the past twenty-five years of her life with such a request. "This could be my chance to experience romance in life, a turning point into an extrovert like all my friends." She steadied herself again and asked, "That's why your face felt so familiar, it is from Mrs. Gerard's student photos. You never shared your name?"

"Oh sorry, I'm Vader. Vader Joshua Jacobson". Creola burst out laughing. Surely the man of her dreams could not be named after the most famous villain of all time. Though a non-believer herself, she could not help thinking about her mother's warning. She was not fooling her mother; she had been fooling herself into believing it. "I'll think about it, you know, after my class tomorrow at Mrs. Gerard's", saying this she quickly left the building, stumbling down the grey stairway dotted with Greek figures on both sides.

The bus dropped her off two streets away from her apartment complex. The fall colors looked beautiful as the cool breeze passed by. Her mother opened the door only after a few knocks, time slowly taking her senses away. Creola could make out a smile under the wrinkles. She told her about the man from the library and her intentions, only to be reminded about the impending warning again.

The next day, Creola left for Mrs. Gerard's weekly lesson dressed her best in a stylish red dress and light makeup. Though her daughter had promised to decline the man's request, her mother still harbored doubt. She was concerned for her daughter's safety and decided to follow her to class. "She is naive to trust strangers who walk up to her. Men are up to no good these days."

Mrs. Gerard's place was only three blocks away. Creola hopped along the footpath humming to music, enjoying the weather, totally unaware of the figure following her in the shadows. Her mother was unable to keep up with the pace. She was old, frail, and her muscles were already sore. This was the most physical activity she had done in years. Her stubborn resolve made up for her physical limitations and she trotted along the sidewalk. Creola went into Mrs. Gerard's apartment, while her mother patiently waited at the bus stop around the corner. The shadows of the concrete jungle lengthened to finally be engulfed by the dark. It was late fall, and the cool wind was unforgiving. Her mother's hands slowly started to freeze. It was then, in the distance, her mother noticed a well-dressed guy walk up to Mrs. Gerard's apartment. "This must be him. I need to warn him not to mess with my daughter and scare him away."

With vigor, she stood up to walk to Mrs. Gerard's porch but unfortunately her poor legs had given away in the cold. She lost her balance, kneeled, and fell face first in front of the bus stop. She was in shock and pain. Her diminutive figure and the dim neighborhood lights made it difficult for the incoming bus driver to spot her on time.

Vader turned around as soon as he heard the screeching brakes of the bus. He observed a crowd huddled around an old woman's bleeding body. He rushed to her side and asked the crowd to move back to allow breathing space. He placed his tuned fingers on the lady's neck to feel for a pulse but to no avail. Guilt swept over Vader. It was not a new feeling to him. For a person in his line of duty as an emergency responder, he was constantly unable to aid people in time. Yet the loss and guilt he felt never diminished, it was what kept him going. Vader tried searching the deceased's coat pockets and found a purse. He shuffled through its contents, finding some cash, coins, an organ donor card, and a photo of Creola.

His heart skipped a beat as he connected the dots. "Maybe her mother was waiting for her daughter to come back from her piano lesson." Tears rolled down his cheeks and his chest pained. He wasn't sure how to convey this to Creola. The emergency vehicles arrived and rushed her to the hospital. Vader did not go along. He wished Creola to have one last nice evening before she learned about her mother's death. He walked back to Mrs. Gerard's apartment and rang the doorbell. Mrs. Gerard's apartment was soundproof, so Vader was sure Creola was unaware of the happenings outside.

Mrs. Gerard opened the door welcoming Vader. He spotted Creola wearing an elegant red dress seated on the piano chair. He waved at her and she waved back excitedly. His heart broke, his eyes watered, but he controlled himself. "Mrs. Gerard, I'll be unable to make it to class today. I'm going out on a date tonight. I was in the area, so I wanted to inform you in person."

Creola wrapped up her lesson and bid goodbye to her teacher. She met with Vader at the porch. "Can I call you Joshua? Vader sounds too intense to me," she chuckled. "Sure, whichever is suitable to you, my lady. Let's go get dinner first, shall we?" "Sure, Joshua. Tell me about yourself first?" As the conversation continued, their minds locked together, then their hands. Her happy ending had arrived.

## After thoughts

Is astrology credible? Human selection bias is what helps outdated ideas such as astrology to live. Moon and other planets having an effect on body water is a ridiculous pseudo-scientific explanation. Tidal forces act due to the radius of the earth. Human emotions are chemical reactions at cellular levels.

It is human nature to find something or someone else to blame for failures. Placing blame on far-away astronomical bodies that do not lash back for being blamed rids the mind of the burden of suffering and inability that is self-inflicted, thus making believers happier with themselves. If we want to prioritize a hedonic lifestyle, then astrology could be said to help towards this. We move away from being truth-seekers to self-deceivers. We have evolved to a place where we have the power to choose between these two options. Is it bad if some choose the other option, or is it better for all to choose the same option for an optimal forward-thinking society instead of being stuck at a locally optimal solution?
