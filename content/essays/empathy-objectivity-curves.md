The unyielding thunderstorm swept over the quaint old town of Salisbury. The rain poured mercilessly down the dilapidated residence of the Warren family. Ada ran towards her bedroom, eyes closed. Her grandmother had shared a mystical story of the gods punishing bad folks by blinding them with lightning. Ada's conscience was clean, yet she did not want to risk losing her eyesight. What if she had done something awful but was not aware of it? The only way to escape punishment was to start repenting immediately.

She ran her little fingers across the length of the door, trying to find the handle in pitch darkness. Pulling open the door with great force, she rushed out, headed in the direction of her mother's bedroom. She fell to the floor when Leo ran right into her. The giant German Shepherd had heard Ada's footsteps and wanted to comfort her. "No, Leo. I don't want to play now," she said worriedly, stroking the soft hair of her favorite being. A stroke of lightning reflected off Leo's glistening eyes.

She stood quietly in the dark, trying to take everything in: the unwavering tick of the analog clock, the shallow breath of Leo at her feet, the sound of rain hitting the roof, and finally her own thumping heart. She no longer wanted to go to her mother. A feeling of astonishment and wonder set in. She had never questioned her decision to run to her mother before. What was different now?

Ada slowly walked back to her bed with newfound reassurance. Reassurance in herself that she could weather this storm on her own. Not because she knew what to do, but because she knew her mother would not be able to do anything more. The feeling of freedom arrives in leaps and bounds when you find a community where no one knows how to take the first step forward.

A leader is born at this forefront of spacetime, to take command of exploration, take the risk of failing, take the risk of squandering valuable time. Some do succumb to this, their tales never told. Few of the fabled tales of defeat do make their way across time. None of them evoke the same feeling as the victorious tales of peasants to philanthropists do.

## Success Stories and Decision-Making

There is magic and charm in hearing success stories. With social media, we have an endless supply of unsolicited content on the meaning and methods of success. Outlier data and fake content abound as they receive validation at scale. The brain pounces upon stitched stories such as these to boost your confidence and conjure up feel-good intuitions in similar scenarios.

There are realms of abstraction that need to be deconstructed to answer fundamental and hard questions, from corporate ethics and government agendas to your personal investment strategy and lunch plan. Your decision-making is crucial to all these areas, yet it is not without support. At the bedrock of decision-making lies philosophy, for truly it is the one thing that all humans are gifted with. The artifacts of civilization, scientific thinking, improvements in quality of life, and technology can all be traced back to philosophy. Animals and plants apply decision-making but without the support of self-generated philosophy.

Humans have evolved into creatures capable of changing our internal philosophy. This is not to say that there is an absolute right or wrong philosophy, but only that we have evolved to be able to change our internal policy.

## Objectivity vs Empathy

This is what happened to Ada as she slowly changed her internal philosophy to be more of an independent thinker, knowing in her heart that her mother would be no better than her in this situation. Another way to spin the same incident would be to cite that Ada did not want to disturb her mother from her peaceful sleep. One reasoning shows the application of objectivity and the other shows the application of empathy. How should one decide which path of reasoning to use: objectivity or empathy?

Objectivity is a tool that has been strongly misused to put forward individualistic ideas that are not coherent. The reasoning should not be open to interpretation. The ground truth should be well-established and accepted. The limitations should be clearly explained and understood.

In the case of empathy, there is no way to differentiate empathy across individuals. Standards and outcomes of empathy could vary greatly, sometimes even directly contradicting each other. There is no way forward since this is a deadlock situation. Empathy cannot be used by itself to justify one approach over another. In fact, empathy would have to borrow objectivity's tenets to be able to stand on strong footing. The primary advantage of empathy is that it has a much more primitive representation due to lack of detail. It is a double-edged sword. The same principle that makes it easier to empathize also makes it harder to rely on in non-traditional scenarios, which is a fundamental effect of information theory.

## Evolution and Competition

As strictly replicating machines on this blue planet, emotional mappings are sufficient to get us from one place to another and help propagate our species. There is no strong reason for the human brain to evolve and become much more capable of handling objective thought than emotional thought. We are already the apex predator and we have no competition in sight, other than intraspecies competition.

Hence we are at a crucial state where our own limitations constrain our improvement. Every human outlier needs to be nurtured if our goal is to expand and improve our domain knowledge and skills. If that is not the goal, then there is a competition-collaboration scenario occurring between empathy and objectivity where we apply one or the other in certain situations to justify actions to ourselves and society.
