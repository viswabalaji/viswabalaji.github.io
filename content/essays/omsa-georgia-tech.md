This post is about my experience with the OMSA program (Online Masters in Analytics) offered by the Georgia Institute of Technology. I finished the required classes in 2 semesters and plan to enroll in the final practicum in the upcoming semester.

I applied to the program in July 2020 for the Spring 2021 semester. Admission results were announced in October 2020. I was confident of getting into the program given my undergrad in computer science and work experience at Microsoft. No standardized test scores were required in the application process (TOEFL may be required for students with no English background).

## Why I Applied

Following are the reasons why I applied to this particular program:

- Being an international student in the US, having a master's degree helps with visa applications like H1B.
- Due to COVID-induced lockdowns, almost all universities were conducting courses online. Georgia Tech is a veteran in the online teaching domain and has established excellent workflows and curriculum.
- The total program cost of $10,000 is an attractive point compared to expensive programs like MIDS offered by UC Berkeley.
- Flexibility to participate in the master's program without giving up my full-time software engineering position. You get the best of both worlds since you do not miss out on the opportunity cost of working in the industry.
- I chose to apply to the OMSA program over OMSCS since OMSA includes a practicum component along with the traditional requirement of 10 courses (3 credits each). As an OMSA student, you can take CS classes you are interested in as well as management courses offered by Scheller School of Business. This is a great option for those interested in dabbling in the management space without directly committing to an MBA.

## Program Structure

The OMSA program requires a total of 10 courses (30 credits) plus 1 practicum (6 credits). I took 5 courses in my first semester (Spring 2021, Jan-May) and the remaining 5 courses in my second semester (Summer 2021, May-Aug). The traditional guidance is to take 1-2 courses per semester, so an explicit advisor override was needed to enroll in 5 courses. I decided to pursue the Computational Data Analytics track and opted out of CS 6040 Computing for Data Analytics.

There is no point in rushing to take multiple courses only to perform in an average manner across all of them. My recommendation is to take as many courses as you are comfortable with while balancing work, and aim to get the highest grade, knowledge, and contentment possible. Knowledge absorbed and contentment reached are difficult to measure, so the final course grade serves as a rudimentary alternative.

I rate the courses below based on cognitive effort, writing effort, and viewing effort.

## First Semester Spring 2021

I enrolled in the following courses (received A in all courses):

| Course | Difficulty | Notes |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| CS 7642 Reinforcement Learning | Difficult | Heavy writing, long videos |
| ISYE 6501 Introduction to Analytics | Easy | Weekly assignments, engaging crisp videos |
| ISYE 6669 Deterministic Optimization | Moderate | Weekly homework, exciting domain |
| ISYE 6420 Bayesian Statistics | Difficult | Biweekly homework, hard to grasp and follow |
| CSE 6242 Data and Visual Analytics | Moderate | Intensive coding assignments, lectures not required |

## Second Semester Summer 2021

| Course | Difficulty | Notes |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| MGT 8803 Business Fundamentals for Analytics | Difficult | 4 exams, information dump |
