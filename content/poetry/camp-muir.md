As I sat perched upon the high top bench at the base camp, I felt a strong urge to stretch my legs, rest my back, look up at the August sky and wonder what got me there.

Definitely my sore legs, very sore from the greatest ascent I've ever made in my life until then. I'm not sure if the altitude had a hand in this, but surely my drive to reach the peak did.

My heart is with the mountain, as strong as it was pounding in my hand during the crevasse filled journey.

The wildlife around only shared my excitement, not my anxiety.

Marmots ran around the meadows trying to find the majestic rocky throne with the backdrop of mighty Rainier while squirrels scuttled under rocks scrounging for food in vivid poses.

More surprising was the presence of an army of bees and single crow at these eternal alpine gardens, unaware of all deceit ice bridges & valiant eddies of glacial water that abounded my journey up and down.

Oh thank my thighs and calves for supporting this burdened mind and body up and down this exquisite beacon of the Pacific Northwest.

As the fiery red sun set on the smoky horizon, I double down on my beverages and food, cheering and cherishing my day. As I patiently awaited for my home to pull up into the view, in the backdrop of Space Needle.
