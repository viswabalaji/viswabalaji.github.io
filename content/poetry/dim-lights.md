A chill runs down my body; from my fingertips to my spine.

My breath heavy from a lifetime of climbing familiar steps, I rest my head back and slowly descend into the warmth of my chair.

This was a place I called home, for I had made a lot of fond memories here. With friends, with family, with acquaintances, the magic of these chairs around never wavered, always striving to bring us together.

It was time for me to bid goodbye to my impressions of the world and suspend my well articulated, head strong opinions and beliefs. Dissolved completely in the ocean of the quails to pave the way for magic.

My muscles loosen, they are no longer needed.
My brain races to catch up to the situation. The lights around me dim, they go almost pitch dark.

For in the final moments of the dim lights, you will see flashes of a thousand lives. The engulfing darkness brightens the ever changing landscape, even if just for a few moments of our life.

Stuck in someone else's dream,
I can't move my arm to hug and comfort my daughter as she cries after falling down a bicycle,
I can't inch forward closer to kiss my date for my fear of rejection reeks in,
I can't shout and warn my friend who is boarding a flight destined to crash.
Perhaps that's how a God would feel.

The only socially acceptable things for me to do are cry, smile, laugh, cringe and clap in this lived dream scape.

Parched throat or a hunger craving interrupt me intermittently, the only indication that I am still alive and kicking.

The chill drink of Coke helps me get back into the groove of the movie, as I kick back and sink into the seat to return to the life I was promised. A life worth with a fifteen dollars.
